Introduction

Russia, officially known as the Russian Federation (RF), is the largest country in the world, spanning Eastern Europe and Northern Asia. It covers more than 17 million square kilometers, nearly twice the size of the United States.

Russia has a rich history, with powerful empires and an influential presence in both European and Asian affairs. It was the leading republic of the Soviet Union (USSR) before its dissolution in 1991.

Russia is a significant player in global politics and has a permanent seat on the United Nations Security Council.

Geography

Location

Russia is located in northern Eurasia, bordering the Arctic Ocean, between Europe and the North Pacific Ocean. Its geography includes vast areas of forests, tundra, and steppes.

Climate

Due to its enormous size, Russia's climate ranges from humid continental in the west to subarctic and polar in the north. Winters are harsh, especially in Siberia, where temperatures can plummet below -50Â°C.

Natural Resources

Russia is endowed with vast natural resources, including major deposits of oil, natural gas, coal, and timber, as well as significant mineral resources such as iron ore and copper.

Economy

Overview

Russia's economy is largely dependent on its natural resources, particularly oil and natural gas. The country's vast energy resources make it one of the largest energy suppliers in the world.

GDP

Russia's GDP in 2023 was approximately $4.32 trillion, ranking it as one of the largest economies in the world by purchasing power parity (PPP).

Major Industries

The Russian economy includes major industries such as mining, manufacturing, and defense. Other key sectors include agriculture, fishing, and forestry.

People and Society

Population

As of 2024, Russia has a population of approximately 143 million people, making it the ninth most populous country in the world.

Ethnic Groups

Russia is home to over 190 ethnic groups, with Russians making up the majority. Significant minority groups include Tatars, Ukrainians, and Bashkirs.

Languages

Russian is the official language of the country, though many minority languages are also spoken. It is one of the six official languages of the United Nations (UN).
